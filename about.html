About

Azad Namazie (they/them) is a writer, artist, and digital archivist from Los Angeles, CA. They have a MLIS from UCLA.

Azad's creative practice interweaves memory, mythology, and multimedia technology to explore the poetics of solidarity. Their work has been featured at Celebration Theatre, Zephyr Theatre, and Hollywood Fringe Fest, while their poetry has appeared in Mizna Journal, About Place Journal, and FRUITSLICE, among others. Their plays include Another Birth (commissioned and produced by Celebration Theatre & Greenway Arts Alliance) and LOCAL PROPHETS: A Play of Near Collisions (The Joy Who Lived Festival 2025).

Recent theatrical credits include dramaturgy for Memory Lane is a Desert Road (8 Ball Productions at Zephyr Theatre), written by Nabra & Michael Nelson and directed by Sarah Showich. Azad also curated a pre-show archival exhibition for A Third Space (Hollywood Fringe Festival 2024).

As an archival consultant, they bring experience building digital archives programs at UCLA Library, the Los Angeles County Museum of Art, and Skid Row History Museum & Archive. They enjoy working collaboratively with community-based organizations to build and maintain sustainable, autonomous archives programs that meet their needs and safeguard their materials. Services include metadata modeling, audiovisual digitization, oral history project implementation, and digital preservation planning.

They also coded this website from scratch which explains the extremely crude early internet aesthetic. They have been thinking about disruptions to the technocapitalist world order through concepts like solidarity infrastructures, minimal computing, and "low tech."
