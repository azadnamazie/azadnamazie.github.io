About

Azad Namazie (they/them) is a writer, artist, and digital archivist from Los Angeles, CA.

They work across poetry, performance, and code to build environments that agitate, unearth, and mythologize. Their performance work has been featured at Celebration Theatre, Zephyr Theatre, and Hollywood Fringe Fest, while their poetry has appeared in Mizna Journal, About Place Journal, and FRUITSLICE, among others.

Their plays include Another Birth (commissioned and produced by Celebration Theatre & Greenway Arts Alliance) and LOCAL PROPHETS: A Play of Near Collisions (The Joy Who Lived Festival 2025). Other theatrical credits include dramaturgy for Memory Lane is a Desert Road (8 Ball Productions at Zephyr Theatre) and curation of a pre-show archival exhibition for A Third Space (Hollywood Fringe Festival 2024).

Azad has preserved and made accessible digital collections at the Los Angeles County Museum of Art, Viet Rainbow of Orange County, and Skid Row History Museum. They are a digital archivist at UCLA Library.


This website was hand-coded in html/css. They have been thinking about disruptions to the technocapitalist world order through concepts like solidarity infrastructures, minimal computing, and "low tech."
