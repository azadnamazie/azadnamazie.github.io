About

Azad Namazie (they/them) is a writer, artist, and digital archivist from Los Angeles, CA.

Azad's creative practice interweaves poetry, performance, and multimedia technology to create environments for the desires, griefs, and soveriegnties in the shadow of empire. Their performance work has been featured at Celebration Theatre, Zephyr Theatre, and Hollywood Fringe Fest, while their poetry has appeared in Mizna Journal, About Place Journal, and FRUITSLICE, among others. Their plays include Another Birth (commissioned and produced by Celebration Theatre & Greenway Arts Alliance) and LOCAL PROPHETS: A Play of Near Collisions (The Joy Who Lived Festival 2025).

Recent theatrical credits include dramaturgy for Memory Lane is a Desert Road (8 Ball Productions at Zephyr Theatre) and curation of a pre-show archival exhibition for A Third Space (Hollywood Fringe Festival 2024).

As an archivist, they bring experience building digital archives programs at UCLA Library, Viet Rainbow of Orange County, and Skid Row History Museum & Archive. They enjoy working collaboratively with artists and community-based organizations to build and maintain sustainable, autonomous archives programs that meet their needs and safeguard their materials from resource extraction and proprietary tech. Services include metadata modeling, audiovisual digitization, oral history project implementation, and digital preservation planning.

This website was hand-coded in html. They have been thinking about disruptions to the technocapitalist world order through concepts like solidarity infrastructures, minimal computing, and "low tech."
